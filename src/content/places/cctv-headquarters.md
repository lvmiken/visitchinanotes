The `CCTV Headquarters` is the building that makes people who do not normally care about office towers suddenly stop caring that it is an office tower.

It is strange, self-confident, and unmistakably Beijing.

That is why it matters.

This page was checked against current source material on **June 28, 2026**, including OMA's project page for [CCTV Headquarters](https://www.oma.com/projects/cctv-headquarters), Büro Ole Scheeren's project page for [CCTV](https://buro-os.com/projects/cctv), and the Beijing government's current overview of [Beijing CBD](https://english.beijing.gov.cn/investinginbeijing/InvestmentSpecial/INV_BJ_CBD/). Those sources support the building's looped form, its role as a Beijing icon inside the CBD, and its status as one of the district's defining modern landmarks. I did **not** find a straightforward current public-tourist entry path in the official Beijing travel-facing materials I checked, so the advice below treats the building as an exterior-first landmark. That access conclusion is an editorial inference from current public-facing sources, not a claim of permanent policy.

## The short answer

The `CCTV Headquarters` is usually worth it when:

- you genuinely like architecture
- the trip wants one modern-capital contrast after old Beijing
- the `CBD` already feels like a plausible selective detour

It is usually weaker when:

- the trip is short and tightly classic
- your group only wants attractions with clear public-entry payoff
- the building would become a long standalone errand

## Why this building works so well

Many famous towers are memorable only because they are tall.

The `CCTV Headquarters` is memorable because it does not behave like a normal tower at all.

It works because:

- the looped form is instantly legible
- the engineering feels improbable in person
- the building gives Beijing a modern icon that does not look borrowed from somewhere else

For many first-time visitors, this is the point where modern Beijing stops feeling abstract and starts feeling visually specific.

## Should you plan to go inside?

Usually no.

For most first-time visitors, the smart assumption is:

- exterior-first
- skyline-first
- district context over interior access

If what you really want is a building with a simple ticketed visitor experience, the `CCTV Headquarters` may not deliver that kind of payoff.

If what you want is a building that changes how the city reads, it often delivers very well.

## CCTV Headquarters versus China Zun

Choose [China Zun](/places/china-zun/) when:

- height and skyline orientation are the point
- you want Beijing's tallest tower as the visual anchor
- the trip wants vertical grandeur more than structural drama

Choose the `CCTV Headquarters` when:

- the building's shape is the reason you care
- you want the more radical and immediately recognizable piece of architecture
- you prefer one unforgettable form over one more tall-building superlative

For many architecture-minded first-time visitors, `CCTV` is the building they remember more vividly and `China Zun` is the building that completes the skyline around it.

## CCTV Headquarters versus Olympic Park

Choose [Beijing Olympic Park](/places/beijing-olympic-park/) when:

- you want the easier modern-landmark answer
- the group is mixed
- the visit should feel broader and less architecture-specific

Choose the `CCTV Headquarters` when:

- the building itself is the attraction
- you want sharper skyline personality
- the trip wants a more editorial, less default version of modern Beijing

## How much time does it need?

A realistic `CCTV Headquarters` time budget is:

- `20 to 30 minutes` for a targeted architecture stop
- `30 to 45 minutes` if you want to walk, photograph, and place it properly
- `1 to 2 hours` only if you are giving the wider `CBD` a real role in the day

It usually should not become a half-day mission by itself.

## When does it fit best?

The `CCTV Headquarters` usually fits best:

- on a `4-day` or longer Beijing trip
- after the historic priorities are already secure
- late afternoon into early evening, when the skyline atmosphere improves

If the live question still is not only this building but which one modern branch deserves your remaining time, use [How to Choose One Modern Beijing Detour: CBD, Olympic Park, 798, or Shougang?](/guides/how-to-choose-one-modern-beijing-detour-cbd-olympic-park-798-or-shougang/).

## Who tends to enjoy it most?

The `CCTV Headquarters` is strongest for:

- architecture readers
- photographers
- travelers who want one bold reminder that Beijing is not only an imperial capital but a city with modern design ambition

It is weaker for:

- checklist-only travelers
- visitors who want attractions with a guaranteed interior experience
- short first trips already struggling to fit the essentials

## Common mistakes

- treating the `CCTV Headquarters` like a normal tower you simply go up
- visiting the building without giving the wider `CBD` any context
- adding it too early, before the core Beijing priorities are secure
