Nanjing transport usually is easier than the city first looks on a map.

The problem is not that there are too few options.

It is that travelers mix together:

- station arrival
- major history sites
- old-city evenings
- wider landmark blocks

This page was checked against current official English-language city material on **June 26, 2026**, including the Nanjing government transport hub [Transportation](https://english.nanjing.gov.cn/LivinginNanjing/Transportation/), the official metro how-to page [How to buy tickets for Nanjing Metro](https://english.nanjing.gov.cn/LivinginNanjing/Transportation/202405/t20240508_4660988.html), and the official [Service Guide for Foreigners](https://english.nanjing.gov.cn/LINKS/ServiceGuideforForeigners/202405/P020241213555830235764.pdf), which explains metro, bus, Alipay QR use, and ride-hailing basics for foreign travelers. ## The short answer

For many first-time visitors, the strongest Nanjing transport pattern is:

1. use `metro` for the main city moves
2. use `walking` for the best old-city and evening blocks
3. use `taxi or ride-hailing` for luggage, tired returns, or awkward landmark connections

## When metro is usually the best choice

Metro is usually strongest when:

- you are moving between central districts
- you are arriving from or returning to station or airport links
- the day still is in its broader city-movement phase rather than its final stroll phase

The official Nanjing metro page says tourists can use:

- single-journey tickets
- transportation cards
- the `Nanjing Metro Electronic Card` through Alipay

That makes metro a very practical backbone for many first-time trips.

## When walking should take over

Do not try to solve Nanjing entirely as a transit puzzle.

Walking usually matters most around:

- [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/)
- [Laomendong](/places/laomendong/)
- some central historical blocks once you already are in the area

## When taxi or ride-hailing becomes smarter

For many first-time visitors, paying more is usually worth it when:

- you have luggage
- the return is late
- the weather is poor
- the route is already history-heavy and you want to save energy

That is often especially true after a long day that includes:

- [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/)
- [Nanjing City Wall](/places/nanjing-city-wall/)

## Bus and Alipay help, but usually as support

The official foreigner guide says visitors can use Alipay transport QR functions for both metro and bus in Nanjing after city selection and verification.

That is useful.

But for many first-time visitors, buses are still best as:

- backup support
- short practical connectors

not as the main way to solve the whole city.
