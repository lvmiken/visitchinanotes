If you are landing at **Nanjing Lukou International Airport (NKG)**, the best airport transfer usually is not the one that looks cheapest on a chart.

It is the one that still feels easiest after:

- immigration
- luggage
- a first look at the weather
- and one honest look at how much thinking you still want to do

## The short answer

For most first-time visitors:

- choose **Metro Line S1** if you want the cleanest public-transport answer and do not mind doing the real city transfer at `Nanjing South`
- choose **taxi or Didi** if you land late, have heavier luggage, are traveling as a family, or your hotel still would need one clumsy final leg after the metro
- choose **airport bus** only if one exact city-side drop fits your hotel or first-night plan better than the train

The wrong move is not public transport.

The wrong move is pretending the first arrival should end with one more awkward transfer just because the headline fare looked better.

## Option 1: Metro Line S1

### Best for

- normal-hour arrivals
- lighter luggage
- readers who want the cheapest clean public-transport spine
- hotel bases that can work from `Nanjing South`
- same-day onward train users

For many first-time visitors, `S1` is the default answer because it solves the biggest airport problem cleanly:

it gets you out of airport mode and into a part of the city network that actually makes sense.

That matters because `Nanjing South` is not just a station. It is the point where Nanjing becomes much easier to finish by metro, taxi, or one short decision.

### Why it works

Metro S1 is strongest when:

- you want a predictable cost
- you are still comfortable making one onward connection
- the hotel is not so awkward that a door-to-door car obviously wins

It also is the cleanest answer if the airport is only the first hop and you are continuing straight to or from rail at `Nanjing South`.

If the route already is being built around Shanghai rail logic, keep [Shanghai to Nanjing by Bullet Train: When a Day Trip Still Works](/guides/shanghai-to-nanjing-by-bullet-train-when-a-day-trip-still-works/) nearby too. That page shapes the rail version; this one protects the airport side.

### What to watch out for

Metro gets weaker when:

- you land very late
- the group has several big bags
- the hotel still needs one fuzzy last leg after `Nanjing South`
- you already know the first night should end at the hotel, not with one more station decision

The train only wins if the full arrival still wins.

## Option 2: Taxi or Didi

### Best for

- late arrivals
- heavier luggage
- families
- rainy or very hot arrivals
- old-city hotel bases that still would need one more chain after the train

Taxi or Didi usually is the right answer when the arrival needs to end cleanly.

That matters more when:

- the hotel entrance still is fuzzy in your head
- you do not want to decode one more transfer at `Nanjing South`
- your first Nanjing memory should be a simple hotel check-in, not another underground corridor

If ride-hailing is likely to be your real solution, keep [How to Use Didi in China Without Speaking Chinese](/topics/how-to-use-didi-in-china/) nearby too.

### When taxi often earns its price

Taxi or Didi becomes especially attractive when:

- the hotel is near [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/)
- the hotel is closer to [Laomendong](/places/laomendong/)
- the group is landing tired and still wants the evening to stay usable
- the route needs one cleaner finish before a short `1-night` or `2-day` Nanjing chapter

## Option 3: Airport bus

### Best for

- travelers whose hotel side matches one exact bus drop well
- arrivals where metro no longer feels elegant
- readers who want a public-transport middle ground between train and taxi

Airport bus is not the blind default for most first-time visitors.

But it still can help when one route matches your real hotel side better than:

- riding S1
- transferring at `Nanjing South`
- then still solving one more city leg

### Why it matters

Airport bus becomes most useful when:

- you want a simpler one-seat feeling than metro
- the arrival hour makes a direct-ish city drop attractive
- you already know your hotel side well enough to judge whether the bus really helps

If you cannot picture the final drop clearly, taxi or Didi is usually safer.

## Which option should most readers choose?

### Choose Metro S1 if

- you want the cleanest public-transport answer
- you are traveling light
- the arrival is at a normal hour
- your route can finish neatly from `Nanjing South`

### Choose taxi or Didi if

- you land late
- luggage is heavy
- the hotel still needs a fuzzy final leg
- the old-city evening matters more than saving one more transfer fare

### Choose airport bus if

- one exact city-side drop clearly fits your hotel
- you want a public-transport option but do not want the metro transfer logic
- the arrival hour makes that bus route more attractive than rail

## District shortcut

### Xinjiekou or central Nanjing

Metro S1 plus one onward metro or a short taxi cleanup often works well here because the city-center finish usually stays legible after `Nanjing South`.

If the base itself still is not settled, read [Best Area to Stay in Nanjing for First-Time Visitors](/guides/nanjing-where-to-stay/) next.

### Qinhuai, Confucius Temple, and Laomendong side

Metro can still work, but taxi or Didi often earns its price because the whole point of this side is arrival-ease into a stronger evening atmosphere, not one more transfer puzzle.

### Nanjing South station side

Metro S1 is often the cleanest answer of all if your hotel or onward rail logic is built around `Nanjing South`.

### East-side landmark days

If your first real Nanjing block is going to lean quickly toward [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/) or another broader eastern branch, a taxi cleanup after the airport can still be smarter than insisting on a fully rail-shaped arrival.

## The real first-night mistake

The usual mistake is assuming `cheapest`, `fastest`, and `easiest` are always the same answer.

In Nanjing they often are not.

An S1 arrival can still lose if:

- the last leg is annoying
- the group is too tired for one more decision
- the first hotel was chosen for atmosphere rather than transfer neatness

And taxi can still be the smarter arrival if it removes the one piece of friction you would otherwise remember most.

## Common mistakes

- taking S1 without checking what still happens after `Nanjing South`
- forcing public transport after a late arrival just because it looks cheaper
- treating airport bus like the blind default without understanding the drop
- treating the airport transfer as separate from the hotel base and first evening
- forgetting that a short Nanjing stay is usually weakened more by one messy arrival than by one slightly higher transfer fare
