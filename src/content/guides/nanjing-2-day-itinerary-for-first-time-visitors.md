This is the Nanjing version that usually feels strongest for first-time visitors.

If Day 1 begins with a same-day flight rather than a hotel wake-up inside the city, keep [Nanjing Lukou Airport to City Center for First-Time Visitors: Metro S1, Taxi, or Airport Bus?](/guides/nanjing-lukou-airport-to-city-center/) open too, because a messy arrival can steal exactly the time this itinerary needs most.

If the route already looks right but the next problem is which hotel, train, or major history stop deserves early commitment, keep [What to Book in Advance for Nanjing: Hotels, Trains, and Key History Stops](/guides/what-to-book-in-advance-for-nanjing/) open too.

## Day 1: central history plus a real evening

Start with one main historical anchor:

- [Presidential Palace](/places/presidential-palace/) if you want the clearest republican-era story
- or [Nanjing Museum](/places/nanjing-museum/) if you want the broader museum answer

Do not try to make both equally heavy on the same first day unless you already know museums are the trip's core language.

In the late afternoon, shift toward:

- [Laomendong](/places/laomendong/) if you want a softer old-city block before dinner
- then [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/) for the evening

If the live question already is not whether the old-city evening belongs but how to stop it from doing too much, the cleaner route page is [One Good Nanjing Night: Qinhuai, Laomendong, and When Not to Overbuild It](/guides/one-good-nanjing-night-qinhuai-laomendong-and-when-not-to-overbuild-it/).

If the trip wants one heavier modern-history anchor instead, [Nanjing Massacre Memorial](/places/nanjing-massacre-memorial/) can take one protected half day, but it usually works only if the rest of that day stays much lighter.

## Day 2: one broader landmark block

Choose one main anchor:

- [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/) if you want one major symbolic and landscape-linked half day
- or [Nanjing City Wall](/places/nanjing-city-wall/) if you want one more flexible physical-history answer

If Day 2 already has enough room and the trip wants deeper imperial history rather than a simpler landmark answer, [Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum](/places/ming-xiaoling-mausoleum/) is the better expansion than randomly adding one more minor stop.

If the live choice still is not whether Purple Mountain belongs at all but which version of it should carry the day, the cleaner chooser page is [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum or Ming Xiaoling: Which Purple Mountain Stop Fits a First Nanjing Trip Better?](/guides/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum-or-ming-xiaoling-which-purple-mountain-stop-fits-a-first-nanjing-trip-better/).

If the route instead is becoming too formal, [Xuanwu Lake](/places/xuanwu-lake/) can do something more useful than another heavy attraction by giving the day a little breathing room.

If what the day needs is not only a lighter place but a softer half-day shape, the practical route page is [Jiming Temple and Xuanwu Lake: A Calmer Nanjing Half Day When the City Needs Air](/guides/jiming-temple-and-xuanwu-lake-a-calmer-nanjing-half-day-when-the-city-needs-air/).

If the Purple Mountain side already is winning and the live question is how much of it should survive before the city gets swallowed by monuments, the route page is [Purple Mountain Without Losing the Whole Day: Sun Yat-sen, Ming Xiaoling, and What Not to Stack](/guides/purple-mountain-without-losing-the-whole-day-sun-yat-sen-ming-xiaoling-and-what-not-to-stack/).

If Day 1 used [Presidential Palace](/places/presidential-palace/), Day 2 can still fit [Nanjing Museum](/places/nanjing-museum/) only if energy and interest stay high.

## What not to do

- do not make both days equally dense
- do not leave the Qinhuai side until the trip is already tired
- do not stack every major heavy-history site just because the city is compact
