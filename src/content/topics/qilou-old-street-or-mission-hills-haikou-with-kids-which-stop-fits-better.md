This is the real Haikou family comparison.

Not:

`What is famous?`

But:

`What kind of stop does this family actually need right now?`

Because `Qilou Old Street` and `Mission Hills` are not two versions of the same day.

They solve different problems.

This page was checked against current official Hainan material on **June 29, 2026**, including the Hainan government feature [Qilou: Culture and history live on in this century-old street](https://en.hainan.gov.cn/englishsite/oex/202605/9e79f4e0705c42cc96cf44b0eaf080be.shtml?ddtab=true), which frames Qilou through Haikou's trade history and Nanyang-style architecture, the Hainan service listing [Qilou Old Street in Haikou](https://en.hainan.gov.cn/englishsite/ttra/list.shtml), which describes it as the largest and best-preserved group of Nanyang-style arcade buildings in China, Mission Hills China's official resort page [MH Resort Haikou](https://www.missionhillschina.com/en/resorts/mh-resort-haikou/), which currently highlights pools, a water park, an artificial beach, a kids' club, and extensive hot-spring facilities, and the official [Movie Town Haikou](https://www.missionhillschina.com/en/movietownhaikou/) page, which describes a family-oriented entertainment district built around `93` heritage-style buildings. I am using those sources to establish what each branch officially is; the family-fit judgment below is editorial.

## Who this page is for

Use this page if:

- your family is keeping `Haikou`
- you need to choose between central Haikou and `Mission Hills`
- the children might enjoy one softer walk more than one bigger leisure day, or vice versa
- you are trying to stop one Haikou stop from turning into two mismatched half-days

## The short answer

Choose **Qilou** if:

- the family wants one lighter evening
- snacks, strolling, and atmosphere are enough
- the Haikou stop should stay short and selective

Choose **Mission Hills** if:

- the family wants contained leisure
- one child-friendly facility block would genuinely help
- mixed ages need a lower-friction environment

Choose **neither** and go south if:

- the island stay is short
- nobody really wants Haikou
- you are only trying to honor the airport

That is the cleanest rule.

## What kind of family day each one creates

`Qilou` creates:

- a city walk
- food choices
- lighter stimulation
- one easier arrival-evening or half-day

`Mission Hills` creates:

- pools or water facilities
- more contained time
- less urban walking
- a leisure-shaped family block

This is why comparing them by fame usually goes nowhere.

## Choose Qilou when the family needs less, not more

Qilou often wins because it is edited.

It works best when the family needs:

- one gentle first evening
- one place to walk and eat without too much structure
- one Haikou reason that does not hijack the trip

If that branch is already winning, the narrower child page is [Is Qilou Old Street in Haikou Worth It With Kids?](/topics/is-qilou-old-street-in-haikou-worth-it-with-kids/).

## Choose Mission Hills when the family needs a real support day

Mission Hills wins when the family needs more than a walk.

It works best when:

- one child needs water time
- adults want contained logistics
- grandparents or mixed ages would benefit from lower-effort leisure
- the route genuinely has room for one bigger Haikou day

If that branch is already winning, the narrower child page is [Mission Hills Haikou With Kids: Worth It or Too Much Resort Time?](/topics/mission-hills-haikou-with-kids-worth-it-or-too-much-resort-time/).

If the resort branch is winning but the live question is even narrower and really means `is Movie Town itself enough for our kids`, go next to [Is Movie Town Haikou Worth It With Kids?](/topics/is-movie-town-haikou-worth-it-with-kids/).

## Which one fits arrival day better

`Qilou` usually fits arrival day better.

Why:

- it asks less
- it is easier to use for one meal and one walk
- it does not require turning Haikou into a full leisure chapter

`Mission Hills` is usually better when Haikou includes the next day too, not only the landing evening.

If that landing logic is still the real question, pair this with [After Landing in Haikou With Kids, Should You Stay the Night or Go Straight to Sanya?](/topics/after-landing-in-haikou-with-kids-should-you-stay-the-night-or-go-straight-to-sanya/).

## Which one fits a short Hainan trip better

On a short Hainan family trip, `Qilou` usually fits better.

It gives Haikou one reason to exist without asking the island to become broader than it should.

`Mission Hills` becomes stronger only when the family truly wants a leisure branch and has enough time for it.

## Which one fits mixed ages better

This depends on the kind of mixed ages.

Choose **Qilou** if mixed ages still enjoy:

- walking slowly
- browsing
- easier food choices

Choose **Mission Hills** if mixed ages need:

- seating
- contained facilities
- lower-effort scheduling
- a more resort-contained environment

## The question that clarifies everything

Ask this:

`Are we trying to add one softer Haikou mood, or are we trying to create one family recovery block before Sanya?`

If the answer is `softer mood`, choose `Qilou`.

If the answer is `recovery block`, choose `Mission Hills`.

If the answer is `neither`, keep moving south.

## The editorial default

For many first-time families:

- default to `Qilou` if Haikou is only a short support stop
- upgrade to `Mission Hills` only if the family genuinely wants contained leisure badly enough to protect the time

That default works because most families overbuild Haikou faster than they expect.

## Common mistakes

- trying to do both on a short stay
- choosing Mission Hills because it looks safer without asking whether the family actually needs a big leisure complex
- choosing Qilou while secretly expecting a headline attraction day
- forgetting that `go straight to Sanya` is still a valid answer if neither Haikou branch earns the time
