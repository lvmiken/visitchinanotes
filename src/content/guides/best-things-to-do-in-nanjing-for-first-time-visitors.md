Nanjing usually rewards structure more than volume.

If the shortlist is becoming real and the next problem is which pieces deserve early commitment, keep [What to Book in Advance for Nanjing: Hotels, Trains, and Key History Stops](/guides/what-to-book-in-advance-for-nanjing/) open too.

## The strongest first-time Nanjing shortlist

### 1. Protect one evening around [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/)

This is often the block that gives Nanjing its most human and memorable after-dark identity.

### 2. Choose one major historical anchor

For many first-time visitors, that means either:

- [Presidential Palace](/places/presidential-palace/) if you want the clearest republican-era political history
- [Nanjing Museum](/places/nanjing-museum/) if you want the strongest broader museum answer

If the trip also needs one serious modern-history stop, handle that separately and deliberately through [How to Visit the Nanjing Massacre Memorial as an English-Speaking Visitor](/guides/how-to-visit-the-nanjing-massacre-memorial/).

If the bigger question comes one step earlier and you still are deciding whether that stop belongs in your version of Nanjing at all, start with [Nanjing Massacre Memorial: When It Belongs on a First Trip](/places/nanjing-massacre-memorial/).

### 3. Add one broader outdoor or landmark block

Usually choose:

- [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/) if the trip wants one major symbolic hill-and-history layer
- [Nanjing City Wall](/places/nanjing-city-wall/) if the city still needs one physical historical perspective without another museum-style session

### 4. Use [Laomendong](/places/laomendong/) selectively

Laomendong often works well as:

- a companion to the Qinhuai side
- a lighter old-city extension

It is usually weaker as a stand-alone top-three priority.

### 5. Use [Xinjiekou](/places/xinjiekou/) or [Zhonghua Gate](/places/zhonghua-gate/) only when the route needs practicality or one more southern old-city layer

These places often improve the trip, but they usually are supporting pieces rather than the first names to protect.

### 6. Use [Xuanwu Lake](/places/xuanwu-lake/), [Jiming Temple](/places/jiming-temple/), or [Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum](/places/ming-xiaoling-mausoleum/) only when the city already has enough structure

These are all real places, but they solve different second-layer problems:

- [Xuanwu Lake](/places/xuanwu-lake/) when the trip needs breathing room
- [Jiming Temple](/places/jiming-temple/) when the day needs one calmer supporting stop
- [Ming Xiaoling Mausoleum](/places/ming-xiaoling-mausoleum/) when the city needs deeper imperial history rather than one more easy famous name

## What not to overforce

Nanjing usually gets weaker when you try to:

- do every heavy history site at full strength
- turn both daytime and evening into nonstop seriousness
- reduce the city to a rushed side trip

## Best version by trip length

If you have `1 day`:

- choose one major historical anchor
- choose either one outdoor anchor or the Qinhuai evening
- do not try to do everything

If you have `1 night`:

- protect the evening around [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/)
- the city usually starts feeling much more complete

If you have `2 days`:

- Nanjing becomes much more comfortable
- the city can support palace, museum or mausoleum, and an evening without collapsing into fatigue
