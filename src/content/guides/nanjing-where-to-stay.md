Nanjing usually feels best when the hotel helps both the day and the evening.

## Best overall: central Nanjing / Xinjiekou side

This usually is the strongest default if you want:

- flexible movement across the city
- easier access to different history layers
- one safe central base on a short stay

For many first-time visitors, this is the least risky hotel answer.

If the district itself still needs a yes-or-no instead of only a hotel label, the next page is [Xinjiekou in Nanjing: Best Base, Easy Night, or Just Practical?](/places/xinjiekou/).

## Best for atmosphere: Confucius Temple / Qinhuai side

Choose this if:

- you want evening atmosphere built into the stay
- [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/) are central to your trip
- the city should feel older and more atmospheric, not only efficient

## Best only when logistics dominate: station-led convenience

Choose this only if:

- Nanjing is extremely short
- the stop is mainly transit-led
- hotel convenience matters more than city feel

For many first-time visitors, this version is operationally easy but emotionally weak.
