Hangzhou is one of the easiest China cities to undersell if you only know it as a soft add-on from Shanghai.

At one day, it feels scenic. At two days, it starts to feel complete. At five to seven days, it can finally hold together the different versions of itself: West Lake, Lingyin, tea-country, quieter museum or old-core time, one second-green branch, and enough breathing room that the city stops feeling like only a postcard detour.

If you want the broader planning layer behind this route, keep the [Hangzhou destination hub](/destinations/hangzhou/) open too. If the live question still is whether Hangzhou deserves this much time, start first with [How Many Days in Hangzhou for First-Time Visitors](/guides/how-many-days-in-hangzhou-for-first-time-visitors/).

## Who this longer Hangzhou itinerary is best for

This version works best if:

- Hangzhou is a real stop, not only a Shanghai side errand
- you care about atmosphere, tea, and walking rhythm as much as named attractions
- you want to understand the city beyond one standard West Lake day
- you would rather deepen Hangzhou itself than automatically add more East China rail hops

If the stay still may shrink back to two days, [A Practical 2-Day Hangzhou Itinerary for First-Time Visitors](/guides/hangzhou-2-day-itinerary-for-first-time-visitors/) is the cleaner default. If Hangzhou still is competing with Suzhou or Nanjing for route space, step back first to [Hangzhou for First-Time Visitors: When the City Is Worth More Than a Quick Add-On](/guides/hangzhou-first-time-guide/).

## Why 5 to 7 days in Hangzhou can work so well

Longer Hangzhou stays fail when travelers keep repeating one idea of Hangzhou:

- one more lakeside walk
- one more symbolic bridge
- one more pretty tea stop with no real structure

They work when the extra days are used for:

- one proper [West Lake](/places/west-lake/) day
- one proper [Lingyin Temple](/places/lingyin-temple/) or [Longjing Village](/places/longjing-village/) contrast day
- one real food, old-core, or museum-texture day
- one softer green or weather-buffer day
- one final slower day that makes the whole stay feel calm rather than padded

That is the difference between `more Hangzhou` and `better Hangzhou`.

## The classic 5-day core

Think of this page as one strong 5-day Hangzhou structure, plus Days 6 and 7 only when they genuinely improve the trip.

### Day 1: Let West Lake explain the city properly

Keep the opening day focused on:

- [West Lake](/places/west-lake/)
- one real scenic rhythm
- one symbolic support stop such as [Leifeng Pagoda](/places/leifeng-pagoda/) or [Broken Bridge](/places/broken-bridge/)
- one easy lake-side or nearby dinner

This is the day Hangzhou should click immediately.

If the live question already is not whether the lake belongs but how to make it work well, use [How to Plan West Lake in Hangzhou Without Rushing](/guides/hangzhou-west-lake-planning/) and [West Lake in Hangzhou: Is It Worth More Than a Quick Loop?](/places/west-lake/).

### Day 2: Use one true contrast day, not a cleanup list

This is the day for:

- [Lingyin Temple](/places/lingyin-temple/)
- or [Longjing Village](/places/longjing-village/)
- and only one supporting meal or softer finish after that

If the live choice already is not whether the second anchor belongs but which one should carry the day, the sharper comparison page is [Lingyin Temple or Longjing Village: Which Hangzhou Second-Day Branch Is Better for First-Time Visitors?](/guides/lingyin-temple-or-longjing-village-for-first-time-visitors/).

![Longjing Village in Hangzhou.](/images/heroes/hangzhou-longjing-village.jpg)

### Day 3: Give Hangzhou one city-texture day

This day should stop the trip from becoming only scenery.

Strong Day 3 versions usually are:

- [Hefang Street](/places/hefang-street/) plus a cleaner meal block
- [Hangzhou Museum](/places/hangzhou-museum/) plus the old core
- [Hubin Pedestrian Street](/places/hubin-pedestrian-street/) if the city wants an easier modern-lake continuation
- one tea-led half day if Day 2 stayed temple-first

If the live question already is not which district texture belongs but how to build one coherent museum-and-old-core version, the cleaner route page is [How to Plan a Hangzhou Museum and Hefang Street Half Day for First-Time Visitors](/guides/how-to-plan-a-hangzhou-museum-and-hefang-street-half-day-for-first-time-visitors/).

### Day 4: Protect one softer branch and let it be genuinely slower

This is where a longer Hangzhou stay becomes broader instead of merely longer.

Choose one of these:

- `green version`: [Xixi Wetland](/places/xixi-wetland/)
- `tea version`: a fuller tea-country day anchored by [Longjing Village](/places/longjing-village/)
- `museum version`: [China National Tea Museum](/places/china-national-tea-museum/) or another weather-friendly culture day

If the green version is winning, keep [Xixi Wetland in Hangzhou: Is It Worth It for First-Time Visitors?](/places/xixi-wetland/) open too. If the tea version is winning, keep [Longjing Tea Country in Hangzhou: How to Avoid the Wrong Stop and Build a Better Half Day](/guides/hangzhou-longjing-tea-country-without-the-wrong-tea-stop/) nearby.

### Day 5: Use one buffer day to make the whole stay feel better

Day 5 should not be another hardest-hitting sightseeing day.

It usually works best as:

- a food-led day
- a lakeside return that uses better pacing and better meals
- a weather buffer if Days 1 to 4 were exposed
- one final neighborhood you skipped earlier, not three of them

This is often the day when Hangzhou starts feeling lived in instead of only consumed.

Day 6 should deepen the trip, not create scenic sprawl.

The key is to choose one appetite:

- more tea
- more food
- more indoor culture
- more recovery

## How to use Day 7 well

Day 7 is the luxury day.

It should be the most personal day or the easiest day.

Good Day 7 uses include:

- one last lake-side morning and unhurried meal
- one weather-buffer day
- one softer tea-room or museum day
- one very simple closing day if the rest of the week already feels complete

If the trip already feels complete by Day 6, Day 7 should be the first thing you simplify.

## Two classic versions of a longer Hangzhou trip

### Version A: Scenic-and-tea Hangzhou

Lean harder into:

- West Lake
- Longjing
- a tea museum or tea-room layer
- one softer Xixi or museum day
- lighter evenings

This version is strongest for travelers who want Hangzhou to function as the gentlest chapter of a larger China trip.

### Version B: Scenic-and-culture Hangzhou

Lean harder into:

- West Lake
- Lingyin
- Hangzhou Museum and old-core texture
- one food day
- one weather-friendly indoor branch

This version usually works better for travelers who like slower cities but still want a clearer cultural backbone.

## What to cut first if you only keep 5 days

Cut in this order:

1. Day 7 luxury layer
2. Day 6 second soft branch
3. duplicate lakeside logic

Do not cut:

- one real West Lake day
- one true Lingyin or Longjing day
- one city-texture day
- one slower buffer day

## Common mistakes on a 5- to 7-day Hangzhou trip

- repeating the same lake logic instead of giving the extra days different jobs
- forcing both Lingyin and Longjing in full when one stronger contrast day would work better
- adding Xixi by default even when the city already has enough greenery
- using the longer stay to justify more transfers instead of better rhythm
- forgetting that longer Hangzhou works because the city gets calmer, not because it gets fuller
