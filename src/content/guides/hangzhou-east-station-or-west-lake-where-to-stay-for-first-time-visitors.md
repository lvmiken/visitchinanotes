This is one of the most practical Hangzhou hotel questions because both answers can be right.

They just belong to different versions of the stop.

`West Lake` usually means:

- a better first-time Hangzhou feel
- easier scenic walking and evening use
- a hotel base attached to the real point of the city

`Hangzhou East Station` usually means:

- easier high-speed-rail timing
- less risk around a late arrival or early departure
- a more transport-led overnight

This page was checked against current official Hangzhou source material on **June 25, 2026**, including [Hangzhou's Railways](https://www.ehangzhou.gov.cn/2018-06/15/c_274611.htm), which describes `Hangzhou East Railway Station` as a major junction for multiple mainline and high-speed railways, [Hangzhou East Railway Station recognized for management](https://www.ehangzhou.gov.cn/2020-01/21/c_269462.htm), which says the station receives about `600,000` passengers a day on average, [DiDi upgrades real-time parking at Hangzhou East](https://www.ehangzhou.gov.cn/2025-04/10/c_293264.htm), which notes upgraded station pickup handling, and the official page [Hubin Pedestrian Street](https://www.ehangzhou.gov.cn/2021-04/02/c_276710.htm), which places that lake-side zone directly on the bank of `West Lake`. These sources support the transport importance of the station and the scenic importance of the lake side, but they do not rank hotel areas directly. The choice below is an editorial route-fit judgment.

## The short answer

For many first-time visitors:

- choose **West Lake** if Hangzhou should feel like a real stop
- choose **Hangzhou East Station** only if rail timing is unusually strong
- choose **West Lake by default** if you are unsure

That is because a short Hangzhou stay usually gets more value from:

- one easier scenic day
- one better evening
- one hotel area tied to the city's real identity

than from sleeping beside the station just because it is on the route.

## Why West Lake is usually the better first-time answer

For many first-time visitors, [West Lake](/places/west-lake/) is still the stronger base because it lets the city start working immediately.

Why it usually wins:

- the city feels scenic right away
- one lake-side meal or evening is easier to protect
- you do not spend the whole stop commuting back toward the actual experience
- even a short stay feels more like Hangzhou than like a rail transfer with a side trip

Choose **West Lake** if:

- the city still needs one real scenic memory
- the stay is `1` or `2` nights
- you want Hangzhou to feel restorative rather than purely practical
- the rail timing is normal enough that the station does not need to dominate the choice

For many first-time visitors, this is the better `whole-trip answer`.

If the live question now is not the station but which lake-side base is best, the narrower next page is [Where to Stay in Hangzhou for the Best West Lake Night Views](/guides/where-to-stay-in-hangzhou-for-the-best-west-lake-night-views/).

## Why Hangzhou East Station can still be the right answer

`Hangzhou East Station` is not a bad answer.

It is a rail-led answer.

Choose **Hangzhou East Station** if:

- you arrive very late by rail
- you leave very early the next day
- the stop is more transport overnight than scenic stay
- you want to reduce risk around bags, timing, and early departures

The station-side answer is strongest when the sentence sounds like:

We mainly need this stop to stay easy around the train.

That can be completely valid.

It just usually is not the best answer when the sentence actually sounds like:

We want to see Hangzhou properly, even in a short version.

## Which area is better on a 1-night Hangzhou stay?

Usually **West Lake**.

That is because even on a `1-night` stay, the trip often still needs:

- one real scenic block
- one easier dinner
- one evening that makes Hangzhou feel different from Shanghai

If the trip is unusually station-shaped, `Hangzhou East Station` becomes easier to defend.

But for many first-time leisure visitors, sleeping near the station protects logistics more than it protects the city.

## Which area is better if you are arriving from Shanghai by high-speed rail?

Usually still **West Lake**, unless timing is awkward.

That is because Shanghai-Hangzhou rail is easy enough that many first-time visitors over-weight the station and under-weight the actual city experience.

If the train arrives at a normal time and the hotel transfer is manageable, the stronger answer usually is:

- sleep where Hangzhou works
- not where the train ends

## Which area is better if the departure is early?

This is where **Hangzhou East Station** gets strongest.

Choose station-side if:

- the train leaves very early
- the group dislikes morning transport uncertainty
- the stop is already more overnight than scenic stay

That does not automatically make station-side better overall.

It just means convenience may finally be doing enough work to win.

## Do not confuse the rail hub with the city experience

This is the main trap.

`Hangzhou East Station` is a very important transport hub.

That does not make it the best first-time hotel area.

The better question usually is:

What is the stop really for?

Often the answer is:

- **West Lake** if the stop still is a real Hangzhou visit
- **Hangzhou East Station** if the stop mostly is a rail-led overnight

## Common mistakes

- choosing the station just because it looks efficient on the map
- underestimating how much more satisfying a lake-side overnight feels
- choosing West Lake only because it sounds romantic when the train timing really is brutal
- acting like both hotel choices solve the same problem
