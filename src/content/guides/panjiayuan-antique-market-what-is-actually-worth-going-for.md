Sometimes that reputation is deserved.

Sometimes it only means the market is more interesting to browse than to build a whole day around.

## The short answer

For many first-time visitors, `Panjiayuan` is worth it when:

- you already know you like flea markets or antique-market atmosphere
- browsing itself sounds enjoyable
- the trip has enough room for one niche detour

It is usually less worth forcing when:

- Beijing is short and still owes you more essential anchors
- shopping only matters when it is clear, efficient, and low-friction
- you do not usually enjoy markets for their own sake

## What Panjiayuan is best for

`Panjiayuan` is usually best for:

- atmosphere
- old-object overload
- browsing without a rigid script
- watching how a market feels rather than proving you found something important

## Who should prioritize it most

This market is strongest for:

- flea-market lovers
- photographers who enjoy urban texture and object density
- travelers who already know the trip needs one less polished stop

## Who can skip it without regret

You can safely skip or downplay `Panjiayuan` if:

- the trip still lacks major first-time essentials
- what you really want is a calmer neighborhood walk or a park
- shopping only matters when it is clear, efficient, and low-friction

## Common mistakes

- going only because the market sounds famous on the internet
- expecting guaranteed treasure rather than market atmosphere
- giving it core-trip status on a short first visit
