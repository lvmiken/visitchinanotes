If you are landing at **Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport (SHA)**, you are in a much easier position than many first-time visitors imagine.

This guide uses current Shanghai Airport Authority transport information published by the Shanghai government and checked on **June 18, 2026**.

## Who this is for

This page is for travelers landing at **Hongqiao specifically**, not for general Shanghai arrival planning.

It is most useful if:

- this is your first China trip
- you want the easiest route into central Shanghai
- you are deciding between metro, taxi, Didi, or airport bus
- you want to know whether Hongqiao changes the arrival strategy compared with Pudong

If you are still deciding between the two Shanghai airports more broadly, keep [Shanghai Airport to City: What First-Time Visitors Should Choose](/guides/shanghai-airport-to-city/) open too.

## The short answer

For most first-time visitors:

- choose **metro** if your hotel route is straightforward and you want the easiest good-value option
- choose **taxi or Didi** if luggage is heavy, the final route is unclear, or you simply want a lower-stress first night
- treat **airport bus** as a supporting option, not the default blind choice

The important difference from Pudong is that Hongqiao is close enough to the center that public transport often feels genuinely practical, not merely cheaper.

## Why Hongqiao is often easier

Shanghai's official airport transport guide says Hongqiao is only about **13 km from the city center**.

That changes the whole arrival logic.

It means:

- taxi rides are shorter
- metro often feels more realistic
- hotel mistakes hurt less than they do after a far-out airport arrival

For many readers, Hongqiao is one of the softest first landings in China.

## Option 1: Metro

### Best for

- lighter luggage
- daytime arrivals
- hotels with a simple metro finish
- travelers who want good value without creating extra stress

The official Shanghai airport guide says:

- **Terminal 1** is served by **Metro Line 10**
- **Terminal 2** is served by **Metro Lines 10 and 2**

### Why metro works especially well here

At Hongqiao, metro is often a genuinely strong first option because:

- the airport is close to the center
- the lines are useful for common Shanghai hotel areas
- the route often stays simpler than a Pudong arrival

### What to watch out for

Metro is still not the best answer if:

- you land tired and want zero ambiguity
- the hotel finish is awkward
- you have several bags

The cheaper route is only the smarter route if the last stretch still feels easy.

## Option 2: Taxi or Didi

### Best for

- heavy luggage
- late arrivals
- families
- first-night hotel routes that are still fuzzy in your head

Shanghai's official airport transport guide says a taxi from Hongqiao to the city center usually takes about **30 minutes** and costs around **CNY 100 to 150**, depending on traffic.

The same official guide says taxi pick-up points are:

- outside **Gate 1** on the arrivals floor of **Terminal 1**
- outside **Gate 4** on the arrivals floor of **Terminal 2**

### Why it works

Taxi or Didi is the better answer when your goal is simply to end the arrival well and get to the hotel with the least thought.

If you are likely to use ride-hailing, keep [How to Use Didi in China Without Speaking Chinese](/topics/how-to-use-didi-in-china/) nearby before the trip.

## Option 3: Airport bus

### Best for

- travelers whose exact hotel area matches a route well
- budget-aware visitors who already understand the final stop logic

Shanghai's official airport guide says there are **12 bus routes** serving Hongqiao Airport.

### Why it is not the blind default

Airport bus can be useful, but it is usually not the easiest first-answer option unless the route fits your destination clearly. For many readers, metro or taxi is easier to reason through.

## What about the Airport Link Line?

This is worth knowing about, but it is not the main downtown answer for most readers.

Shanghai's official transport guidance notes that the **Airport Link Line** is now in operation between the city's two airports. That matters most if:

- you are connecting between Hongqiao and Pudong
- you are heading toward a route that actually benefits from that corridor

For a normal central-Shanghai hotel arrival, metro or taxi is still usually the simpler first decision.

## Which option should most readers choose?

### Choose metro if

- you land at a comfortable hour
- your hotel route is simple
- you want the best value without overcomplicating the arrival

### Choose taxi or Didi if

- you land late
- you have heavy luggage
- the hotel route is still abstract
- you want the easiest possible first evening

### Choose airport bus if

- the route matches your hotel area clearly enough to justify it

## Common mistakes

- assuming every airport arrival in Shanghai feels like Pudong
- taking metro without checking the final hotel leg
- choosing the cheapest route when arrival energy is already low
- overlooking terminal differences between Line 10 and Line 2 access
