Nanjing is one of those cities where the difference between `1 day` and `2 days` changes not only coverage, but also the whole tone of the stop.

## The short answer

For many first-time visitors:

- `1 day` is possible but selective
- `1 night` is usually the minimum version that feels coherent
- `2 days` is often the sweet spot

## Choose 1 day if Nanjing is a historical sampler

One day works if:

- the wider route is short
- Shanghai still is the main east-China base
- you only need a first impression of Nanjing's historical weight

That version usually should protect:

- one major historical anchor like [Presidential Palace](/places/presidential-palace/) or [Nanjing Museum](/places/nanjing-museum/)
- plus one evening or outdoor block, not several

## Choose 1 night if you want the city to feel real

An overnight stay usually wins if:

- [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/) matter
- the route wants evening atmosphere as part of the city
- you do not want the stop reduced to pure museum logistics

## Choose 2 days if Nanjing is one of the route's true city stops

Two days usually are worth it when:

- you want both a major indoor anchor and a major outdoor anchor
- [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/) and [Nanjing City Wall](/places/nanjing-city-wall/) both are in play
- you want the city to breathe
