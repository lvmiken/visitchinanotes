This route works because each stop should solve a different problem.

Shanghai gives you scale, neighborhoods, skyline, and the easiest urban landing. Suzhou changes the pace. Nanjing adds historical weight and a more grounded final chapter.

If all three cities start doing the same job, the route collapses into rail admin.

If Hangzhou still also belongs in the conversation because the route may want a softer scenic branch before Suzhou and Nanjing, step back first to [A 6- to 8-Day Shanghai + Hangzhou + Suzhou + Nanjing Route That Still Feels Edited](/guides/a-6-to-8-day-shanghai-hangzhou-suzhou-and-nanjing-route-that-still-feels-edited/).

## Why this order works

`Shanghai -> Suzhou -> Nanjing` is usually the cleanest order because:

- Shanghai is the easiest arrival city
- Suzhou is the softest transition away from Shanghai's scale
- Nanjing closes better than Suzhou because it can carry a fuller final day and one stronger evening

The sequence also helps emotionally:

- `Shanghai` opens wide
- `Suzhou` exhales
- `Nanjing` deepens

That is more satisfying than bouncing between two cities that both are trying to be `the calm one` or `the serious one`.

## The 5-day version: tight, but still worth doing

Use this version if east China is only one segment of a longer China trip.

### Day 1: Arrive and settle into Shanghai

Keep the first day short and forgiving:

- check into the right base using [Best Area to Stay in Shanghai for First-Time Visitors](/guides/shanghai-where-to-stay/)
- take one easy opening walk around [The Bund](/places/the-bund/) or [French Concession](/places/french-concession/)
- keep dinner and bedtime early enough that the route starts calm

If arrival logistics still are the live issue, use [Shanghai Airport to City Center: Which Transfer Is Best for First-Time Visitors?](/guides/shanghai-airport-to-city/).

### Day 2: Give Shanghai one real city day

Protect one full Shanghai identity rather than several partial ones.

Usually that means either:

- skyline plus old core: [The Bund](/places/the-bund/), [Yu Garden](/places/yu-garden/), [City God Temple](/places/city-god-temple/)
- or neighborhood-led Shanghai: [French Concession](/places/french-concession/) and [How to Plan a Wukang Road and French Concession City Walk in Shanghai for First-Time Visitors](/guides/how-to-plan-a-wukang-road-and-french-concession-city-walk-in-shanghai-for-first-time-visitors/)

If the city still feels broad and underbuilt, use [Shanghai 3-Day Itinerary for First-Time Visitors](/guides/shanghai-3-day-itinerary/) before protecting anything else.

### Day 3: Shanghai to Suzhou

Move in the morning and keep Suzhou elegant, not crowded.

The clean first structure is usually:

- one major garden such as [Humble Administrator's Garden](/places/humble-administrators-garden/)
- then [Suzhou Museum](/places/suzhou-museum/) and [Pingjiang Road](/places/pingjiang-road/)
- then a quieter or brighter evening depending on energy

For the rail version, use [Shanghai to Suzhou by Bullet Train: A Day Trip That Actually Works](/guides/shanghai-to-suzhou-by-bullet-train-a-day-trip-that-actually-works/) even if you are staying overnight; the transport logic still helps.

### Day 4: Suzhou to Nanjing

Do not squeeze a second ambitious Suzhou day and a full Nanjing day together.

Instead:

- keep Suzhou light in the morning if needed
- move to Nanjing by midday
- protect the evening around [Confucius Temple and the Qinhuai River](/places/confucius-temple-and-qinhuai-river/)

If the Nanjing evening still feels vague, use [One Good Nanjing Night: Qinhuai, Laomendong, and When Not to Overbuild It](/guides/one-good-nanjing-night-qinhuai-laomendong-and-when-not-to-overbuild-it/).

### Day 5: Give Nanjing one real historical day

Pick one heavier indoor anchor and one broader outdoor anchor.

Common strong combinations:

- [Presidential Palace](/places/presidential-palace/) plus [Nanjing City Wall](/places/nanjing-city-wall/)
- [Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum](/places/sun-yat-sen-mausoleum/) plus a lighter old-city evening repeat only if energy remains

If Nanjing is the route's emotionally serious city, consider whether [How to Visit the Nanjing Massacre Memorial as an English-Speaking Visitor](/guides/how-to-visit-the-nanjing-massacre-memorial/) belongs. Do not force it only because it is famous.

## The 6-day version: the sweet spot for most travelers

This is often the best first-time version.

It gives you:

- `3 days` for Shanghai
- `1 night / 1 day` for Suzhou
- `2 days / 1 night` for Nanjing

That usually feels right because:

- Shanghai no longer feels only introductory
- Suzhou still stays refined
- Nanjing gets both an evening and a real daytime identity

The extra Shanghai day can go toward:

- [The Bund at Night: Light Timing, Photo Spots, and How to Get the Skyline Right](/guides/the-bund-at-night-light-timing-and-photo-spots/)
- [A Shanghai Coffee Walk Through Anfu, Julu, Fumin, and Changle](/guides/a-shanghai-coffee-walk-through-anfu-julu-fumin-and-changle/)
- or one museum-and-old-core layer if the city still feels too polished and not yet rounded

The extra Nanjing time can go toward:

- [Purple Mountain Without Losing the Whole Day: Sun Yat-sen, Ming Xiaoling, and What Not to Stack](/guides/purple-mountain-without-losing-the-whole-day-sun-yat-sen-ming-xiaoling-and-what-not-to-stack/)
- or [Jiming Temple and Xuanwu Lake: A Calmer Nanjing Half Day When the City Needs Air](/guides/jiming-temple-and-xuanwu-lake-a-calmer-nanjing-half-day-when-the-city-needs-air/)

## The 7-day version: when the route can breathe

At seven days, the route stops being clever and starts being genuinely enjoyable.

A strong shape is:

- `Days 1 to 3`: Shanghai
- `Days 4 to 5`: Suzhou
- `Days 6 to 7`: Nanjing

That version works well if:

- you want Suzhou to include both a daytime cultural block and a proper evening
- you want Nanjing to hold both one serious history block and one softer supporting half day
- the trip prefers rhythm over speed

## What not to do

Avoid these common mistakes:

- giving Shanghai only one partial day and then wondering why the whole route feels thin
- turning Suzhou into two or three gardens that all solve the same problem
- arriving in Nanjing too late to use its evening
- putting every train move in the middle of the most valuable sightseeing hours

The route works best when trains support the structure instead of becoming the structure.

## If you need to cut something

Cut `Suzhou` first if:

- the trip is becoming rushed
- you want more urban energy than softness
- Shanghai and Nanjing already are carrying the route well

Cut `Nanjing` first if:

- you want a lighter east-China route
- heavy historical content is not the reason for the trip
- Suzhou's gentler contrast matters more to you than a stronger second city

Cut `Shanghai extras` first if:

- Shanghai already has two good days and the route genuinely wants both other cities
